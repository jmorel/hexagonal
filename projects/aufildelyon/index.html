Au fil de Lyon

A redesign for better communication
and greater accessibility

The redesign of Au fil de Lyon proved a challenge because of its target audience. Though Lyon is the second largest french city, it is nowhere near Paris in terms of tourism. Tourists flock to the city for its world renowned festival of lights but aren't a year-long driving force in the local economy. As a result, tour guides work mainly for local residents interested in the city's history, politics and town-planning. Sadly enough these people, however brilliant, are often pensioners and less than knowledgeable when it comes to browsing the web.

The main goal of this redesign was therefore not to add eye-candy to an existing structure but rather re-think what information is displayed, how it should be so and how to guide the user through the information without him feeling lost, wondering where to find this or that or even what to do next.

Visit the website.

Specifications

Message

  • Guided tours in Lyon, about Lyon, its history, urbanism and politics.
  • 3 types of tours are offered: for adults, children (with tailored content) and urban hikes.
  • All tours are designed, researched and crafted by Blandine. She is an independant guide fueled by her passion for this city.

Audience

  • Tourists
  • Newcomers to the city or curious inhabitants with little knowledge of the it.
  • More often than not, culture clubs (mostly pensioners).

Content

  • Presentation and contact information
  • List of visit (organized by type)
  • Prices, always searched for.
  • Legal notice

Analysis & shortcomings

  • Visits (the main content) are listed in last position in the menu.
  • Visit titles are non-specific do not allow the potential client to understand what the visit will be about.
  • The legal notice features proeminently in the menu while its page contains only a registration number.
  • The news box says "website is and will always be up to date", and is therefore useless.
  • "Print version" doubles with the brochure.
  • Encoding issues.
  • Feedback is ridden with typos and not informative for the potentiel client.
  • Pictures in the gallery are of low quality and do not showcase neither the city many beauties nor groups having a great time.
  • Visit counter should be replaced by modern analytics.

The Redesign

  • Single page design for easier navigation.
  • The service offered is now the main title.
  • The Lobster Two font ensure a welcoming feeling.
  • Blandine's contact information and personal introduction at the very beginning set the tone and introduce her to the visitor as approachable.
  • Short feedback excerpts emphasis the quality of the visits.
  • Visits are displayed using consistent hierarchy: a photo (as representative as possible), a main title (a few words only, easily distinguishable from the others), a subtitle (a sentence to introduce the visit's topics) and a full length description. All pratical information (duration, meeting point, prices) are available right there, no need to change page.
