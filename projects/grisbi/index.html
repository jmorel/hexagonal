Grisbi

Cookies, curses and cult movies.

In Italy Grisbì are cookies. Delicious, crunchy, creamy cookies. In France however, grisbi is 60's slang for cash and is well known thanks to the kitchen scene of the cult movie Les Tontons Flingueurs: when an inebriated young lady dugs her hand into the (litteral) bag of cash, the (soon to be inebriated) lawyer jumps on her arm, his eyes twitching with anger, shouting Touche pas au grisbi salope !

I'll leave you to your favourite online translation service to get the full meaning of this sentence.

Nota Bene It appears that grisbi also used to be italian slang for cash, as demonstrated in the cult 1975 movie Amici Miei, which features (though in a supporting role this time) Bernard Blier, the bald actor in the hereabove video clip.

With this project, my goal was nothing less than bridging the alps between France and Italy, bringing together the two countries, all for the sake of a pun.
I made a display case for the Grisbì biscuits.
